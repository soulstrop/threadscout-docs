ThreadScout User Guide

This guide explains the intent and value behind the ThreadScout reseller experience. For technical setup and installation, see DEPLOYMENT.

1. The Core Reseller Loop

Goal: Convert raw photos into a structured listing in under 60 seconds.

Traditional listing requires manual typing of brand, size, and material. ThreadScout uses AI Vision (Gemini 2.0 Flash) to “see” the garment just like a buyer does.

  • Value: Reduces cognitive load and manual data entry by ~80%.
  • Intent: The AI is your first draft. It provides a structured starting point that you simply verify and refine.

2. Market Research & Enrichment

Goal: Optimize listing quality and pricing using the best of the web.

Great listings aren’t just accurate; they are enriched with catalog data and competitive pricing.

  • Visual Research: Use the Images button to find retail/catalog matches. This ensures you have the correct model name and MSRP.
  • Market Research: Use the Sold button to see what people are actually paying.
  • Enrichment: The Paste & Scrape feature allows you to ingest high-fidelity data from matching URLs, instantly upgrading your listing from a basic description to a professional catalog entry.
    • Security Note: For your protection, ThreadScout only accepts URLs from public, non-internal websites. URLs pointing to local or private network addresses are blocked.

3. eBay Integration

Goal: Eliminate the “copy-paste” tax.

Once your metadata is finalized, ThreadScout pushes it directly to your eBay account as a draft.

  • Value: No more switching between tabs to recreate the listing on eBay.
  • Intent: ThreadScout handles the heavy lifting of category matching and policy application, so you can focus on inventory turnover.

4. Mobile Experience: Share to ThreadScout

Goal: Get photos from your phone to the ingest queue without manual transfers.

Reselling starts with your phone’s camera. ThreadScout integrates directly into your device’s native sharing menu.

  • Mobile Uploads: Use the Upload button in the gallery to pick photos directly from your phone’s camera roll.
  • Android: Install the ThreadScout PWA and use the Web Share Target. Select your photos in Google Photos, click “Share,” and choose ThreadScout.
  • iOS: Use the provided ThreadScout Shortcut. Once installed, it adds a “Send to ThreadScout” option to your iOS share sheet for multi-photo uploads.
  • Value: Eliminates manual transfers. Your photos are ready in the gallery before you even sit down at your desk.

5. Beta Onboarding & The Waitlist

Goal: Ensuring a high-quality experience for early adopters.

ThreadScout is currently in a “Managed Beta” phase to ensure stability and cost-efficiency. It utilizes the ThreadScout Unified Design System (TSDS) to provide a professional, high-contrast experience that is accessible across all devices.

  • Identity & Security: ThreadScout uses a separate identity provider (e.g., auth.threadscout.com). When you click login, you will be redirected to this subdomain to authenticate securely with your Passkey or email. Once authenticated, you’ll be redirected back to the main dashboard.
  • Identity Linking (SPEC-40): You can link multiple social accounts (e.g., Google, eBay, Apple) to a single ThreadScout profile. This ensures you never lose access if one provider is unavailable. Link additional providers in the Admin dashboard.
  • Account Recovery: If you lose access to your primary Passkey, use a linked social provider or your recovery email (configured in Admin) to regain access.
  • Invitation Only: Access is currently granted via unique invitation tokens.
  • The Waitlist: If you don’t have a token, join the waitlist directly on the landing page. We release new slots in waves.
  • Guided Tutorial: New users see a 3-step onboarding tour (AI Vision, Enrichment, and eBay Integration). You can revisit this tour anytime from the Admin dashboard.

6. Storage & Bring Your Own Storage (BYOS)

Goal: Maintain control and scalability of your binary data.

ThreadScout supports multiple storage backends to suit your scale.

  • Local Storage: Default for personal use; images stay on your hard drive.
  • BYOS (S3/R2): Pro users can connect their own S3-compatible buckets (like AWS S3 or Cloudflare R2). This offloads binary storage from your local machine to the cloud.
  • Security: Your storage credentials are encrypted at-rest and never returned in plain text to the UI.

7. Operational Resilience (Manual Failover)

Goal: Maintain reseller productivity even if automated services (AI, eBay, APIs) are temporarily unavailable.

ThreadScout is a tool, but your business shouldn’t stop if it’s “down.” Follow these manual failover procedures to keep listing.

7.1. AI Vision Failover (Extraction)

If the AI cannot “see” your garment (e.g., Vertex AI outage):

  1. Manual Foldering: Create a new folder in your storage/ directory using the naming convention: [brand]-[item-type]-[color]-[unique-id].
  2. Manual Metadata: Open the metadata.json file in that folder (or create one) and manually fill in the brand, size, material, and condition fields.
  3. UI Refresh: The ThreadScout dashboard will pick up these manual changes upon the next refresh.

7.2. Research & Enrichment Failover

If “Paste & Scrape” or deep linking fails:

  1. Google Lens: Upload your “Hero Shot” directly to Google Lens.
  2. Manual Comping: Search eBay’s “Sold” listings directly using the identified brand and model name.
  3. Synthesis Editor: Manually type the discovered MSRP, model name, and description into the ThreadScout Synthesis Editor. Your data is still siloed and organized for the future.

7.3. eBay Integration Failover (Drafting)

If “Push to eBay” fails (e.g., eBay API maintenance):

  1. Export Metadata: Use the “Copy to Clipboard” buttons in the Synthesis Editor for the Title and Description.
  2. Manual Draft: Open the eBay “Create Listing” page. Paste the Title and Description from ThreadScout.
  3. Manual Photos: Drag and drop the photos from your local storage/{user_id}/{item_id}/ folder directly into the eBay listing editor.

7.4. Mobile Ingest Failover

If the PWA Share Target or iOS Shortcut fails:

  1. Cloud Sync: Use Google Photos, iCloud, or Dropbox to sync photos from your phone to your computer.
  2. Desktop Upload: Use the “Upload” button on the ThreadScout Dashboard (Desktop) to select the synced photos.
  3. Manual Transfer: Move the photos directly into the ingest/ directory on your host machine. The backend watcher will process them automatically.

8. Getting Help & Reporting Issues

Every operation in ThreadScout generates a Debug Snapshot.

  • The Snapshot ID: Found at the bottom of the Synthesis Editor. This is a unique “time-machine” for your listing’s state.
  • Reporting: Use the “Report Issue” button in the sidebar. This opens a modal where you can describe the bug or performance issue.
  • Automatic Context: When you report an issue, ThreadScout automatically attaches the latest Debug Snapshot ID and your browser/OS information to the report.
  • Ops Pulse Dashboard: Developers and advanced users can triage these reports directly in the system’s dedicated “Ops Pulse” dashboard (/ops), ensuring high-fidelity reproduction and faster resolution times.

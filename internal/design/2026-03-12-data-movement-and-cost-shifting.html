Design: Data Movement and Cost-Shifting

Date: 2026-03-12
Status: Accepted
Authors: Architecture Squad

1. Executive Summary

As a highly visual, image-heavy application, ThreadScout’s primary operational cost—alongside AI inference—is bandwidth and storage. Moving 12-megapixel raw .heic or .jpg files across network boundaries is expensive and latency-inducing.

To maximize margins and ensure a snappy user experience, ThreadScout employs a “Boomerang Data Movement” and “Cost-Shifting” architecture. The core philosophy is: ThreadScout is the keeper of the metadata and relations; the User and the Marketplaces bear the burden of bulk storage and transmission.

This document outlines how we minimize the number of bits transferred, optimize data locality during processing, and shift the final upload payload to the user’s edge network.

2. Core Architectural Principles

  1. Edge-to-Marketplace Egress (The Boomerang): ThreadScout does not act as a passthrough proxy for sending high-resolution images to eBay. The final, optimized images are returned to the User’s device, which then directly uploads them to eBay Picture Services (EPS). This shifts the heaviest bandwidth cost to the user’s WiFi or cellular network.
  2. Locality of Processing: Once raw images hit the ThreadScout ingest server, all heavy processing (resizing, U-Net background masking, C2PA signing) happens locally. We only egress highly optimized, downscaled images to external AI APIs (Gemini, Nano Banana).
  3. Tiered Storage & Upsells: By default, ThreadScout is ephemeral for large binary blobs. The user keeps the original raw images on their device, and eBay keeps the final enhanced images on their servers. If users wish for ThreadScout to act as a permanent cloud backup or perform direct server-to-server eBay uploads, this is offered as a premium, metered cloud-service upsell.
  4. Multi-Marketplace Future-Proofing: Because different marketplaces (Poshmark, Depop, Mercari) have different aspect ratio and background requirements, the User Device will likely want to retain copies of both the raw and the processed images. ThreadScout maintains the relational mapping (Item -> Raw Image Hash -> Processed Variations) in its lightweight metadata database.

4. Edge Cases & Optional Upsells

While the “Boomerang” pattern is the default path designed for maximum cost efficiency for ThreadScout, there are scenarios where users may want a different architecture:

4.1 The “Fully Managed” Cloud Tier

For high-volume sellers who do not want to tie up their phone’s bandwidth or local storage, ThreadScout can offer a Managed Hosting Tier.

  • Action: Instead of routing the final images back to the user’s device, ThreadScout’s servers push the optimized binaries directly to an S3-compatible bucket and then directly to the eBay EPS API.
  • Business Model: ThreadScout resells AWS S3 storage and egress bandwidth at a premium markup.

4.2 Multi-Channel Distribution

When ThreadScout expands to platforms beyond eBay (e.g., Poshmark, Mercari), the metadata database becomes the crucial nexus.

  • ThreadScout stores the relational mapping: Item ID -> {Raw Image, White Background Edit, Hero Edit}.
  • Because the user has saved the enhanced photos back to their local device (as shown in the flowchart), the ThreadScout client app can simply orchestrate the distribution of the locally-held optimized assets to the respective marketplace apps or APIs, again bypassing ThreadScout’s server egress costs.

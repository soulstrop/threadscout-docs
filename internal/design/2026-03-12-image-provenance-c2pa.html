ThreadScout Image Provenance & AI Authenticity

Date: 2026-03-12
Status: Accepted
Authors: Architecture Squad

1. Executive Summary

ThreadScout relies heavily on visual data to automate the clothing resale workflow. Users upload raw photographs, which the system then processes, enhances (via background removal), and analyzes using generative AI (Gemini Vision) to extract structured product data (brand, size, material, condition).

As AI tools become more prevalent in modifying imagery, maintaining trust on secondary marketplaces (like eBay) is paramount. Buyers need assurance that the item they see in a photograph accurately represents the physical item they will receive. To address this, ThreadScout implements a transparent Image Provenance Pipeline utilizing the Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity (C2PA) standard.

This document outlines our strategy for balancing the efficiency and aesthetic benefits of AI image manipulation with the critical need for cryptographic authenticity.

2. The Problem Space

2.1 The Need for Manipulation

Raw photos ingested from mobile devices are rarely “marketplace-ready”:

  • Clutter: Backgrounds (bedrooms, floors) distract buyers and trigger algorithm penalties on platforms like eBay.
  • Orientation: Overhead flat-lays often lack reliable EXIF rotation data.
  • Cost/Latency: Sending 12-megapixel raw images to an LLM for data extraction is slow and token-inefficient.

Therefore, ThreadScout must manipulate these images (resize, rotate, mask backgrounds) to operate effectively and produce high-converting listings.

2.2 The Risk to Trust

When a system automatically removes a background or “enhances” a photo, it introduces the risk of misrepresenting the item. If a background removal algorithm accidentally clips a tear on a shirt, or if an AI enhancement smooths out a stain, the buyer is deceived.

We need a standardized way to say: “This image was resized and the background was removed by AI, but the pixels representing the actual garment were untouched.”

3. Design Decisions

3.1 C2PA (Content Credentials) as the Standard

We have adopted the C2PA standard (via the c2pa-python SDK) to cryptographically sign every image manipulated by ThreadScout.

Why C2PA?

  • Industry Standard: Backed by the Content Authenticity Initiative (CAI), it is the emerging standard supported by major hardware manufacturers and software platforms.
  • Granular Assertions: It allows us to specify exactly what was changed (e.g., c2pa.resized, c2pa.removed.background) rather than a binary “AI Generated” flag.
  • Tamper-Evident: Cryptographic signatures ensure that if a user manually downloads a ThreadScout photo and doctors a stain out in Photoshop, the signature breaks, breaking the chain of trust.

3.2 Non-Destructive Storage Architecture

Transparency requires a verifiable source of truth.

Decision: ThreadScout enforces a strict non-destructive storage model. When an image is ingested to the siloed storage (storage/{user_id}/{item_id}/), it is saved in a /raw/ directory exactly as received from the device (preserving the original hash and EXIF data).

The C2PA-signed, processed images used for the final eBay listing are stored in a parallel /processed/ directory. The C2PA manifest embedded in the processed image explicitly references the original raw image as an “ingredient,” creating a verifiable audit trail back to the untouched original.

3.3 Strict “No In-Filling” Policy for Subjects (With One Exception)

While ThreadScout aggressively manipulates backgrounds for the primary gallery images, we impose a strict architectural boundary on the subject (the clothing item itself).

Decision: For standard gallery photos, we do not use generative AI to in-fill, upscale, or “heal” the pixels of the actual garment.

  • Background removal uses deterministic masking (e.g., U-Net segmentation).
  • Any AI used on the image is purely analytical (Gemini Vision extracting data) rather than generative.

The “Hero Image” Exception: To maximize marketplace CTR, ThreadScout generates one purely synthetic “Hero” image per item (visualizing the garment on a translucent or stylistic mannequin via Nano Banana). This specific image does involve generative AI altering the presentation of the garment.

  1. It is used strictly as the primary search-index image (the cover of the book).
  2. It is explicitly watermarked/declared as an AI visualization in the metadata and description.
  3. The remaining gallery images adhere strictly to the “No In-Filling” rule.

4. The Processing Pipeline Flow

From a technical perspective, the provenance story unfolds automatically upon ingestion:

  1. Ingest & Preserve: The mobile app uploads the raw .heic or .jpg. It is saved to /raw/. If the original file already has a C2PA manifest (e.g., from a modern smartphone camera), it is preserved as the foundation.
  2. Optimize & Orient: The backend resizes the image (to optimize LLM token costs) and corrects rotation.
    • Action appended: c2pa.resized, c2pa.orientation.changed
  3. Mask Background: A specialized model segments the garment and replaces the background with a neutral solid/black color.
    • Action appended: c2pa.removed.background
  4. Hero Generation (Branch): One optimized image is routed to a Generative Model to synthesize the “worn on mannequin” Hero shot.
    • Action appended (Hero Only): c2pa.created.generative.ai
  5. Sign & Embed: The c2pa-python service compiles these actions, signs them using ThreadScout’s X.509 certificate, and embeds the manifests into the final .jpg assets.
  6. Analyze (Read-Only): The signed, optimized images are sent to Gemini Vision to extract the item’s brand, size, and condition.

5. Summary

By strictly separating the raw source of truth from the marketplace-optimized asset, and by cryptographically linking them via C2PA, ThreadScout provides a robust defense against misrepresentation. Buyers (and marketplace algorithms) can verify that while the presentation was enhanced for clarity, the physical condition of the garment remains authentic to the original capture.

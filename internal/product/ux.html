ThreadScout: Product Vision & UX Design

ThreadScout is not just a tool; it is a Reselling Operating System. This document defines the “Soul” of the application—the vision, the design principles, and the user-centric journeys that drive every technical decision.

1. The Core Vision: “Reselling, Reimagined”

The traditional reselling workflow is plagued by a “Cognitive Tax”: the repetitive, manual extraction of data from physical items into digital forms. This tax leads to listing burnout and limits inventory turnover.

ThreadScout’s Mission: To eliminate the friction between “Taking a Photo” and “Making a Sale” through an intelligent, local-first automation layer.

Core Pillars

  1. Invisible Effort: The AI should feel like a silent partner, not a complex configuration task.
  2. Sovereign Data: Resellers own their inventory data. We prioritize local-first storage and private execution.
  3. High-Fidelity Research: Moving beyond “guessing” to “knowing” by bridging the gap between a photo and the global market.

2. Design Language & Brand Identity (TSDS)

ThreadScout’s visual identity communicates Stability, Intelligence, and Efficiency. It is formalized in the ThreadScout Unified Design System (TSDS), built on Tailwind CSS v4 for a high-performance, CSS-first implementation.

Visual Identity & Tokens

ThreadScout uses a high-contrast utility palette optimized for both bright photography studios and low-light warehouses.

  • Color Palette:
    • Action Blue (#2563eb): Primary brand color for interactive elements and AI operations.
    • Deep Indigo (#1e3a8a): Provides structural stability and professional depth.
    • Incident Red (#dc2626): High-visibility semantic for errors and critical alerts.
    • Research Purple (#7c3aed): Used for AI enrichment and market research context.

The “Character Blueprint” (Design Logic)

To maintain visual coherence between the mascot and the utility UI, we adhere to three foundational principles:

  1. Uniform Line Weight: The mascot’s ink outlines share the exact pixel-width as the border-2 (Tailwind) used on UI Cards and Buttons, creating a shared visual reality.
  2. Semantic Fill Logic: The mascot is never colored arbitrarily; his palette is strictly derived from the TSDS functional tokens, reinforcing the meaning of color throughout the app.
  3. Adaptive Ink: Through CSS currentColor binding, the mascot’s outlines flip from black (Light Mode) to off-white (Dark Mode), ensuring he feels like a native part of the interface rather than a static image.

UX Principles

  • Adaptive Contrast: The UI automatically shifts between Light and Dark modes based on system preferences, ensuring legibility across diverse lighting environments.
  • Atomic Consistency: Every interactive element (Button, Input, Badge, Card) is a standardized atomic component, ensuring a predictable user experience regardless of the platform.
  • Skeleton-First Feedback: We utilize shimmering skeleton loaders during AI analysis to improve perceived performance and maintain layout stability.

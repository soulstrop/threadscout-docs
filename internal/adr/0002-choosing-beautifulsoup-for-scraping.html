ADR 002: Choosing BeautifulSoup over Playwright for Enrichment Scraping

Status

Accepted

Context

In ADR 001, we decided on a “Paste & Scrape” workflow for item enrichment. We then needed to decide on the technical implementation for fetching and parsing the external HTML.

The primary candidates were:

  1. BeautifulSoup4 (with HTTPX): A lightweight HTML parser that works on static HTML.
  2. Playwright: A full headless browser capable of executing JavaScript and bypassing complex anti-bot measures.

Decision

We chose Option 1: BeautifulSoup4 with HTTPX.

Initial investigations into target sites (eBay, rrr.lv) showed that primary product metadata—specifically Titles and Descriptions—are present in the raw, server-side rendered HTML.

Rationale

  • Performance: BeautifulSoup is orders of magnitude faster than spawning a headless browser instance.
  • Resource Efficiency: A full browser stack (Playwright/Chromium) requires significant memory and CPU, which is excessive for parsing static text.
  • Complexity: BeautifulSoup has a smaller dependency footprint and is easier to maintain within a standard FastAPI/Python environment.
  • Reliability: For the specific resale sites identified as primary research targets, static parsing is sufficient to retrieve the “Gold” data needed for AI enrichment.

Consequences

  • Positive: Very fast enrichment loop; low overhead on the local development environment.
  • Negative: The scraper may fail on “Single Page Applications” (SPAs) or sites that require heavy JavaScript execution to display content.
  • Future Work: If we find that a significant number of user-provided research sites are inaccessible via static scraping, we will revisit this decision and consider adding a Playwright fallback.
